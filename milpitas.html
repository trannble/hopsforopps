STEMgirls Elementary STEM Outreach Program: Pomeroy

Location: Pomeroy Elementary School

A free, afterschool program where 4th-6th graders get exposure to different STEM subjects through interactive lessons. Lessons happen once a month and include activities like 3D printing, atom model building, lava lamp, catapult-building, and more!

CoderDojo

Location: SJCC Milpitas Extension

CoderDojo is a global volunteer-led community of free programming workshops for young people between 7 and 17. The Milpitas extension is based in the the SJCC Milpitas Extension. Youths visiting our Dojo will learn to build websites, creative applications, and explore technology in a creative and social environment.

School Year

Coding4Kids

Location: Pomeroy Elementary School

Coding4Kids is a nationwide nonprofit initiative that is dedicated to fostering a love of coding in young students and introducing youth to the tech industry. We have expanded to two schools and are expanding to more in the 2019-2020 school year. Our mission is to spread awareness of the tech industry to as many elementary students as we can.

Learn More
Summer

Robotika Summer Camp

Location: SJCC Milpitas Extension

Robotika Summer Camp is a weekly program for elementary and middle school students that teaches students about robotics (both computer science and mechanical engineering), building and programming robots in small teams, and competing with these robots at the end of the 6 weeks. They will be taught the basics of C++ and will implement their new skills on robots and digital projects.

Learn More
Summer

Read Write Discover

Location: Milpitas Library

Read Write Discover (RWD) is a completely free, all volunteer-run nonprofit organization, serving over 300 K-8th graders every summer. Read Write Discover builds upon each child’s individual literacy skills and pairs them with a dedicated tutor for an individualized learning experience, working with peers near their skill level.

Learn More
Summer

MATHeasy

Location:Milpitas High School

MATHeasy is a FREE math class/tutoring program for K-12 students, supported by high school and college tutor volunteers. The mission of MATHeasy is to help K-12 students succeed in math. With a strong foundation, students can achieve other great things in the future.

Year Round

Recreation Assistance Program (RAP)

Location: Varies

The Recreation Assistance Program (RAP) provides eligible Milpitas families with matching financial assistance to participate in Milpitas Recreation and Community Services programs. RAP allows adults, children and seniors the opportunity to have quality recreational experiences that may not be possible without matching financial assistance.

Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program

Location: Varies

A free 7-week introductory computer science program for 10th-11th grade girls going into their junior or senior year of high school. During the 7-week program, participants learn about computer science, gain exposure to tech jobs, and join a sisterhood of girls who are using computer science to become change-makers.

Summer

Milpitas Youth Force

Location: Varies

A Summer Teen Work-readiness program, Milpitas Youth Force (MYF), where each individual teen will placed at an interested work site. The purpose of MYF is to prepare Milpitas teens, ages 15-18, to successfully and confidently enter the workforce by providing new opportunities, goal setting, hands on exposure, breaking down barriers and career soft skill building.
